The Transformer is a deep learning architecture developed by Google researchers in 2017, introduced in the paper "Attention Is All You Need".
Unlike recurrent neural networks (RNNs) that process data sequentially, Transformers process all parts of the input data simultaneously.
This parallelization significantly reduces training times and allows the model to handle long-range dependencies in text much more effectively.
The core mechanism of the Transformer is "Self-Attention", which allows the model to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence relative to one another.
Modern large language models, including GPT-4, Llama 3, and Claude, are all based on the Transformer architecture.
The architecture consists of an Encoder and a Decoder, though many modern models (like the GPT series) are decoder-only architectures optimized for next-token prediction.
